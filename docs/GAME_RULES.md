# Auxchamp Game Rules

This document defines the game as a rulebook. It describes how a game is set up, how rounds are played, how players participate, how stars are awarded, and how winners are determined.

## 1.0 OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Purpose

This rulebook defines the standard rules of play for the game.

### 1.2 Nature of the Game

A game is a multi-round competition built around themed track submissions and player voting.

### 1.3 Participants

Each game has one Creator and one or more invited Players.

### 1.4 The Creator

The Creator organizes the game, manages invitations, starts the game, and may exercise the powers granted to the Creator by this rulebook. The Creator also participates as a normal Player.

### 1.5 Round Structure

A game contains one or more rounds. Each round follows the same core rules.

## 2.0 OBJECTIVE

### 2.1 Round Objective

In each round, Players submit tracks that fit the round's theme, then award stars to the submissions they judge strongest.

### 2.2 Game Objective

The objective of the game is to finish with the highest total number of stars across all rounds.

### 2.3 Round Victory

The round winner is the Player whose submission or submissions receive the most stars in that round.

### 2.4 Game Victory

The game champion is the Player with the most total stars across the full game.

## 3.0 TERMS

### 3.1 Creator

The Creator is the Player who creates and administers the game.

### 3.2 Player

A Player is a participant in the game, including the Creator.

### 3.3 Round

A Round is one complete cycle of setup, submission, voting, and scoring.

### 3.4 Submission

A Submission is a track entered by a Player for a round.

### 3.5 Star

A Star is one vote awarded to one submission.

### 3.6 Eligible Player

An Eligible Player is a Player who is currently allowed to act in the stage in question.

## 4.0 GAME SETUP

### 4.1 Creator Authority Before Start

Before the game starts, the Creator prepares the game and its rounds.

### 4.2 Required Setup Elements

Before the game starts, the Creator sets the following:

- the number of rounds
- the submission window length
- the voting window length
- the number of submissions allowed per Player
- the starting roster from the invited Players
- each round's theme or title
- any round description or prompt the Creator wishes to include

### 4.3 Round Definition

Each round must have a theme or title.

### 4.4 Optional Round Prompt

Each round may also include a description or prompt.

### 4.5 Minimum Requirements to Start

A game may not start until it has at least:

- 4 active Players, and
- 1 round

### 4.6 Start of the Game

The game begins only when the Creator starts it.

## 5.0 MEMBERSHIP

### 5.1 Invitation Only

Players join a game by invitation only.

### 5.2 Joining Before Start

Before the game starts, invited Players may join the game according to the Creator's setup.

### 5.3 Joining After Start

After the game has started, a new Player may join only by invitation and with the Creator's approval.

### 5.4 Leaving the Game

A Player may leave the game at any time.

### 5.5 Removal by the Creator

The Creator may remove a Player from the game.

### 5.6 Rejoining the Game

A Player who has left the game may rejoin only with the Creator's approval.

### 5.7 Immediate Participation After Join or Rejoin

If a Player joins or rejoins while a stage is still open, that Player may participate in that stage immediately.

### 5.8 Effect of Leaving or Removal on Past Actions

A submission, ballot, or earned stars already recorded before a Player leaves or is removed remain part of the game's history and results.

### 5.9 Effect of Leaving or Removal on Current Eligibility

A Player who leaves or is removed may not continue to participate in the current stage or in later stages unless they rejoin under the rules of the game.

### 5.10 Removal Before Acting in the Current Stage

If a Player is removed during an open stage before acting in that stage, that Player immediately loses eligibility for that stage.

## 6.0 STAGES

### 6.1 Standard Stage Order

Each round proceeds through the following stages in order:

1. Setup
2. Submission
3. Voting
4. Scored

### 6.2 Setup Stage

During Setup, the round exists but is not yet accepting submissions.

### 6.3 Submission Stage

During Submission, Eligible Players may enter their tracks for the round.

### 6.4 Voting Stage

During Voting, Eligible Players award stars to the round's revealed submissions.

### 6.5 Scored Stage

During Scored, voting is closed, stars are counted, and round results are final.

## 7.0 SUBMISSIONS

### 7.1 Submission Limit

The number of submissions each Player may enter in a round is set when the game is created.

### 7.2 One Rule Set Across Rounds

The same submission rules apply to every round in a given game.

### 7.3 Optional Note

A submission may include an optional note.

### 7.4 Editing During Submission

A Player may edit or replace that Player's own submission or submissions until the Submission stage closes.

### 7.5 Hidden During Submission

Submissions remain hidden while the Submission stage is open.

### 7.6 Reveal at Start of Voting

When Voting begins, all submissions for that round are revealed for voting.

### 7.7 Anonymous During Voting

When submissions are revealed for voting, the identity of the submitting Player remains hidden until Voting ends.

### 7.8 Notes During Voting

Submission notes are shown during Voting.

### 7.9 Responsibility for Anonymous Notes

Players are responsible for not using submission notes to reveal their own identity.

### 7.10 Missing a Submission

A Player who does not submit within the Submission stage misses the chance to submit for that round.

### 7.11 Voting Without Submitting

A Player may still vote in a round even if that Player did not submit in that round, so long as that Player is otherwise eligible to vote.

## 8.0 VOTING

### 8.1 Expectation to Vote

All Eligible Players are expected to vote while the Voting stage is open.

### 8.2 Ballot Size

Each ballot must contain exactly 3 stars.

### 8.3 Distinct Targets

The 3 stars must be assigned to 3 different submissions.

### 8.4 No Self-Voting

A Player may not award a star to any of that Player's own submissions.

### 8.5 Multiple Submission Games

If a game's setup allows multiple submissions per Player, the self-voting rule applies to all of that Player's submissions.

### 8.6 Editing During Voting

A Player may change that Player's own ballot until the Voting stage closes.

### 8.7 Missed Vote

A Player who does not cast a ballot before Voting closes simply forfeits that vote.

### 8.8 Hidden Ballots During Voting

While Voting is open, who voted for what remains hidden.

### 8.9 End of Voting Privacy Change

When Voting ends, submitter identities and ballot ownership may be revealed as part of the round results.

## 9.0 SCORING AND WINNERS

### 9.1 Value of a Star

Each star is worth 1 point.

### 9.2 Round Scoring

A Player's round score is the total number of stars received by that Player's submission or submissions in that round.

### 9.3 Multiple Submission Scoring

If a game allows multiple submissions per Player, all stars received by that Player's submissions in the round count toward that Player's round score and game total.

### 9.4 Round Winner

The round winner is the Player with the highest round score.

### 9.5 Round Ties

If two or more Players finish a round tied for the highest round score, all tied Players are round winners.

### 9.6 Cumulative Standings

Game standings are based on each Player's total stars across all scored rounds.

### 9.7 Game Champion

The game champion is the Player with the highest total number of stars when the game ends.

### 9.8 Final Ties

If two or more Players finish the game tied for the highest total, all tied Players are co-champions.

## 10.0 PROGRESSION AND TIMING

### 10.1 Manual Start

The Creator always starts the game manually.

### 10.2 Time Windows

Each round uses the game's submission window and voting window to determine how long those stages remain open.

### 10.3 Automatic Advancement by Deadline

If a stage reaches its deadline, the game may advance according to the rules of that stage.

### 10.4 Early Advancement by the Creator

The Creator may advance a stage before its deadline only when the minimum conditions for the next stage have been met.

### 10.5 Submission to Voting Minimum

A round may not move from Submission to Voting until the round contains enough submissions for every Eligible Player to cast a valid ballot under the Voting rules. In standard play, this requires at least 4 submissions.

### 10.6 Voting to Scored Early

The Creator may move a round from Voting to Scored before all Eligible Players have voted. Any Player who has not voted by that moment forfeits that vote.

### 10.7 Too Few Submissions at Deadline

If the Submission stage reaches its deadline without enough submissions to permit valid voting, the round does not move to Voting.

### 10.8 Pause for Creator Decision

When a round has too few submissions at the deadline, the round pauses and waits for the Creator to decide whether to grant an extension and, if so, for how long.

### 10.9 Future-Only Timing Changes

If the Creator changes submission or voting window lengths after the game has started, those changes apply only to future stages or future rounds, not to the stage already in progress and not to any completed round.

## 11.0 CHANGES AFTER THE GAME STARTS

### 11.1 Locked Current and Past Rounds

Once the game has started, the current round and all past rounds are locked.

### 11.2 Editable Future Rounds

Once the game has started, the Creator may still edit future round themes or titles and future round descriptions or prompts.

### 11.3 Membership Changes After Start

After the game has started, the roster may still change through invitations, removals, departures, and approved rejoins.

## 12.0 COMPLETION

### 12.1 End of the Game

The game ends when the final round reaches the Scored stage.

### 12.2 Final Results

Once the game ends, the results are final.

### 12.3 Historical Record

Past submissions, ballots, and stars remain part of the completed game's record.
